STUDY WITH ME

The New Studying Tool

Study with me is a product designed for students to study or learn together in the same place at any time. It helps students stay organized while studying, giving them easy access to their notes and other resources while also, keeping them motivated.

This application gives students of all ages easy access to the notes they have created and saved. It allows them to create the notes and edit them however they like while also have giving them the option to have favourite notes and find any particuar note they want to use. Study with me also recommends notes for the student to study, reminding them that they need to go over the notes if they have not read them in a while.

Features

    Create Notes

    This feature allows you to create new notes. By clicking the ADD button at the bottom right corner of the screen, new notes are created, once the SAVE button is clicked. If a new note is not titled, the system automatically gives it the title 'Untitled'.

Edit Notes

The student is allowed to edit their notes to suit their preferences. The UPDATE button applies and saves changes made to the note. The INSERT button inserts pages either before or after a certain page in the note, depending on what the student wants. The DELETE button deletes pages in the note. Once the final page of the note is deleted, the entire note is deleted.

Find Notes

This feature allows the student to search for and find a particular note by using the SEARCH icon found in the top left corner of the main page. This is possible as long as the note that is being searched for has been created and saved on the app's system.

Favourite Notes

The student can have favourite notes that can be easily accessed through the ⋮ button at the top right corner of the main page. A note becomes a favourite note when the ☆ button, at the bottom of any page, is selected.

Recommended Notes

This feauture provides the student with the notes that have not been opened within seven days, that is, the system notifies the student that they have not read the note in a while and it is recommended that they go over it soon. Recommonded notes can be found when the ⋮ button, at the top right corner of the main page, is clicked. There is also recommended favourite notes, which are recommended notes that were added to favourites.

Development Experience

  • Improvement from Iteration 2

    We tried to ensure we outlined the most important tasks to be completed before the end of the iteration and assigned them to ourselves as early as we possibly could. We also set deadlines for ourselves to ensure that we move on to the next task as early as possible, so that our goals could be completed on time.

  • What we would do differently

    If we had the chance to start over, we would aim to start our projects earlier because, although we got most of our work done, we often found ourselves still working on last minute details for the project the day before.

  • Project change from Iteration 0

    During our planning for the project, we had a lot of big ideas. We wanted to have games on the software, an achievement bar, multiple learning resources, like an in-built pomodero method timer or an active recall feature, but we soon realised that all these would take a lot of time and that was something we did not have. We had to drop a lot of features but we were able to carry-out some of our vision.

Learn More

Study with me is a product that allows students to study and learn alone or together in the same place at any time. This product is to help students stay organised while studying, giving them easy access to their notes, reminders, flashcards, learning resources, and quiz games, while also keeping them motivated to learn more.
The application is primarily designed for students. It will permit them to create sections for their courses by creating chapters and other categories to keep their notes organized. The students will be able to view, edit, delete notes. They would also be able to make notes as a group at the same time or different times so that they would be to read what they may have missed during a class. They would also be able to find specific notes.

Students would be able to get reminders for the things they are supposed to do like assignments or quizzes that are due, midterms that are coming up, and projects that they need to finish.They would also be able to set reminders for study sessions. This is to help them remember the things they have to get done before it is past the due dates. The students will be able to decide when they want these reminders to be sent to them and how often it would be repeated.

The software would be able to suggest which of the notes, written by the student,should be read based on how often the student reviews it. The least-read note would be highly recommended because it would tell the student that they have not finished their work in that note or that they have not read it in a while and they need to finish their work or go over what they have learned. Their most-read notes would be least recommended as they heavily review these notes.

The product will also allow students to make flashcards and play games amongst themselves. The flashcards will be made by the students and it would be available for use whenever they want to use it. They can also be used in a game, where the students can go answer the questions on the cards and be awarded with points if they get it right. This allows them to challenge themselves. With the flashcards,the students will also be able to review what they have learned so far. Leaderboards will be created to encourage them to acquire more knowledge so they can get to the top or remain at the top.

This product is designed with the idea of making learning and studying easy and fun for students. It is to provide all the resources that they need in one place, so that it would be manageable for them and it would make it easy for them to stay up-to-date.

The product is considered successful if the average of the star rating is 3 or above out of 5, which is to be determined by a survey that would be conducted by 10 university students and 10 non-university students.
